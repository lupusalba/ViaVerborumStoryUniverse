## I. META & BASE RULES

### 1. Genre, Audience, Tone

- **Genre:** Space-fantasy / HFY / void-magic progression
- **Format:** Novel, **6 main episodes/chapters**, 60,000-80,000 words
- **Audience:** Adult males (25-45), progression fantasy/HFY readers
- **Tone:**
    - Competence porn
    - Stoic, pragmatic, tactical
    - No melodrama, no "power of friendship" speeches
    - Violence, torture, and body horror present but handled clinically, not edgelord
    - Dark humor as coping mechanism
    - Emotional beats earned, not manipulative

### 2. POV & Style

- **POV:** 3rd Person Limited, locked to **John** (no head-hopping)
- **Register:** Clean, direct sentences; technical when dealing with magic/tactics
- **Voice:** Military precision with occasional dry humor; compartmentalized emotions
- **No physics jargon:**
    - Treat magic as **structure & flow**, not as real physics
    - Use terms like "pressure, flow, pattern, geometry, structure" rather than "quantum, entropy, etc."

### 3. Core Premise (One-Sentence)

> A wounded Human Alliance special forces veteran from a mana-dead "Death World" is sold and gifted to an ancient Dark Elf Archmage in a mana-rich empire, evolves into a void-walking superweapon through tactical innovation and engineering pragmatism, and hijacks his master's tower to escape into the unknown wilderness.

## II. UNIVERSE & FACTIONS

### 1. Macro Setting – The Divide

- The universe is split into two broad regions:
    
    - **Dead Zones**
        - No ambient mana
        - Home of the **Human Alliance** on so-called "Death Worlds"
        - Humans evolved via environment + tech, not magic
        - Physically dense, adaptable, aggressive problem-solvers
        - Technology-based civilization (terraforming, advanced weapons, space warships)
    - **Mana-Rich Zones**
        - Saturated with ambient mana
        - Home of the **Magic Galactic Empire** and many old magical species
        - people mostly use spells, formations, flying ships, and gates
        - Magic-based civilization (formations, artifacts, living constructs)
- **Status of Humans:**
    
    - Recently discovered by the wider galaxy
    - Extreamly wide teritory (terraformed planets and spike in population, technological revolution)
    - Negotiating entry into galactic-level politics
    - Considered exotic, semi-mythical "Dead Zone natives" by the Magic Empire
    - Small presence in border regions; no official presence in deep Empire territory

### 2. Local Region – Carina Anadeia & Planets

- **Carina Anadeia Galaxy**
    - Border galaxy of the Magic Galactic Empire
    - no official Human Alliance presence
    - Remote enough that news travels slowly
- **Black Market Planet**
    - Small, mana-active world used as a holding pen and auction block for illegal trade
    - Houses pens for exotic species like humans
    - Operates in legal gray area (Empire tolerates it for intelligence purposes)
    - Where John is initially sold by pirates
- **Casil's Planet**
    - Lush, beautiful, **mana-dense** environment
    - Society relies on:
        - Teleportation gates (fixed network between cities)
        - Large formations (infrastructure, defense, agriculture)
        - Flying ships (mana-powered, magic tech-based)
        - Magical infrastructure (living constructs, automated systems)
    - Politically under the **Magic Galactic Empire** with a central ruler / imperial system
    - Casil's tower is located in **wilderness**, hours away from nearest city

### 3. Local Society

- **Noble Races**
    - Elves and Dark Elves at the top; naturally high mana sensitivity and control
    - Long lifespans (300-500 years base, extended by circle advancement)
    - Control most high-level positions in magic, research, and governance
- **Mages**
    - Build **magic circles** inside their mana pool
    - Control spellcasting, research, and high-level politics
    - Progression measured by circle count (1st-9th)
    - Most mages plateau at 3rd-4th circle
- **Knights**
    - Weaker in fine mana control
    - Don't build circles, build Aura Core
    - Circulate mana through their bodies to strengthen muscles, bones, nerves
    - Generate **Aura** for martial techniques
    - Generally cap at equivalent of 5th-6th circle power
- **Technicians / Artificers**
    - Smiths and enchanters who build magic items, gate artifacts, experimental constructs
    - Attempt (usually fail) pocket-dimension work
    - Most are 2nd-4th circle mages with specialized knowledge
    - Produce space artifacts for non-space mages (teleportation items, storage rings, etc.)

### 4. Power Hierarchy

- **Royal Magic Tower**
    - Central institution of magical research and control
    - Monopolizes high-circle knowledge (6th-9th)
    - Controls strategic assets (void mages, ancient artifacts, ley-line networks)
    - Politically powerful; can override local governors
- **Planetary Governor**
    - Manages local administration and law
    - Typically a 5th-6th circle noble mage
    - Answers to Empire, coordinates with Royal Tower
    - The one who gifted John to Casil
- **Independent Archmages**
    - High-circle mages (6th-7th+) who maintain private towers
    - Technically under Empire authority, but largely autonomous
    - Expected to contribute research or service to Empire
    - Casil is one of these

## III. MAGIC & EVOLUTION SYSTEM

### 1. Mana & Evolution

- **Mana as Evolution Reagent**
    - Mana exposure forces **mutation and adaptation** at the cellular and spiritual level
    - Over generations, species either:
        - Evolve and stabilize (most Empire species)
        - Collapse and die out (failed evolutions)
    - Mana is not just energy; it's a transformative force that rewrites biology
- **Humans – Empty Vessels**
    - Human bodies are **highly compatible** with mana but structurally fragile:
        - They can absorb an enormous amount of mana (far more than evolved species)
        - Their "shell" (body + soul integration) is brittle; if mana volume surpasses what the shell can integrate, it cracks → death
    - Humans are effectively **"blank-slate" evolvers**:
        - They haven't settled into a final, stable mana-form as a species
        - They can steer their individual evolution with intent and method
        - If they fail to evolve properly, they die
    - **The Paradox:** Humans have massive potential but no natural defenses against mana poisoning
- **Evolution Process**
    - **Without Knowledge:** Takes generations of random mutation; most individuals die
    - **With Knowledge & Resources:** Can be done in years through deliberate training and mana exposure
    - **Critical Threshold (0.9 → 1.0):** The deadliest moment; shell must fully integrate or it shatters, body must evolve, soul must strenghten.
    - John has access to the best magical resources on the planet, making rapid evolution possible
- **John's Evolution Path (Conceptual)**
    - **Ep1 – Human v0.2:** Injured, fragile, no mana adaptation
    - **Ep2 – v0.3-0.5:** Arms healed, bones denser, nervous system beginning to adapt
    - **Ep3 – v0.5-0.7:** Mana channels forming, shell stabilizing with pocket dimension
    - **Ep4 – v0.7-0.9:** Full "battle body"; 3rd circle; shell reinforced via pocket dimension
    - **Ep5 – v0.9-0.99:** Approaching critical threshold; shell stress visible
    - **Ep6 – v1.0:** 5th circle, internal pocket dimension stabilized; "Void Walker" baseline achieved

### 2. Affinity & Absorption Rules

- **Standard Rule (Everyone Else)**
    - Mages absorb mana **closest to their elemental affinity** efficiently (fire, water, light, etc.)
    - Mana far from their affinity is slow, wasteful, and hard to refine
    - Multi-affinity mages are rare and highly valued
    - Affinity determines spell efficiency and advancement speed
- **Human Anomaly – John**
    - **Universal High Efficiency Absorption:**
        - John can absorb Fire, Water, Earth, Light, Darkness, Void, etc. with roughly equal efficiency
        - His total capacity feels bottomless and keeps expanding
        - He refines all mana types quickly, which is considered impossible
    - **Casting Bottleneck:**
        - Despite absorbing everything, he **cannot cast** non-void spells
        - Attempts at Fireball or similar just result in mana dispersing uselessly
        - The mana structures refuse to cohere outside his body except for void/space magic
        - He is strictly a **Void/Space Caster**
    - **Why This Matters:**
        - Makes him seem defective to Casil initially ("leaking" mana, can't cast basic spells)
        - Actually makes him a perfect void battery (can fuel spells with any mana type)
        - Means he has no inherent weakness to any element (can absorb enemy attacks)
- **Dispersion & Healing**
    - John invents **Mana Dispersion**:
        - Controlled venting and circulation of mana through and out of his body
        - Practiced during calisthenics and strength drills
        - Not sustainable indefinitely, but effective as a training method
    - Effects:
        - Acts as continuous, high-intensity regeneration and strengthening mechanism
        - Repairs his nerve-damaged arms within days
        - Reinforces bones, muscles, tendons, nervous system
        - Gradually adapts his body to handle higher mana concentrations
    - Casil misreads dispersion as "leakage" and initially downgrades John as a defective vessel

### 3. The Circle System (Mage Progression)

- **Concept**
    - Mages build nested **magic circles inside their mana pool**:
        - Strengthen the shell (body-soul integration)
        - Compress mana (more power per unit volume)
        - Shorten casting times (pre-formed spell structures)
        - Improve stability and spell complexity
    - Each circle is a geometric pattern etched into the mana pool itself
    - Higher circles require exponentially more precision, knowledge, and mana control
- **Tiers:** 1st → 9th Circle
    - **1st-3rd Circle:** "Inner Circles" – Foundation stage
    - **4th-6th Circle:** "Middle Circles" – Expert stage
    - **7th-9th Circle:** "High Circles" – Archmage stage
- **Lifespan Bonuses**
    - 3rd Circle: +20% lifespan (~60 extra years for humans)
    - 6th Circle: +50% lifespan (~150 extra years)
    - 9th Circle: +>50% lifespan (open-ended / legendary; some live 1,000+ years)
- **Difficulty Thresholds**
    - **3rd → 4th:** First major wall; requires true understanding of mana theory
    - **6th → 7th:** Massive wall; requires paradigm shift in understanding
    - **7th → 8th:** Near-impossible; requires resources and insights most Archmages never obtain
    - Each threshold eliminates 80-90% of mages who attempt it
- **Knowledge Control**
    - **1-3 Circles:** Theory widely known but tightly supervised by academies and institutions
    - **4-5 Circles:** Accessible through higher academies or noble backing; requires mentorship
    - **6+ Circles:** **Strictly controlled** by the **Royal Magic Tower**; few have access
    - **7-9 Circles:** Essentially state secrets; Royal Tower guards this jealously
- **John's Trajectory**
    - Learns theory for 1-5 circles from Casil's library (one of the most complete private collections)
    - Reaches:
        - **1st Circle:** ~Year 1 (during experiments, before he realizes it)
        - **2nd Circle:** ~Year 2 (during library study)
        - **3rd Circle:** ~Year 3 (after pocket dimension success)
        - **4th Circle:** ~Year 4 (during oath period)
        - **5th Circle:** ~Year 5 (just before betrayal discovery)
    - Faces almost no bottlenecks; this terrifies Casil
    - His void affinity + universal absorption + "blank slate" evolution = perfect conditions

### 4. Void / Space Magic

#### 4.1 Rarity & Role

- Space/void mages are **extremely rare** (maybe 1 in 100,000 mages)
- Treated as precious strategic assets by all major powers:
    - Mostly used as **luxury couriers** for VIPs
    - Gate-network specialists (maintain teleportation infrastructure)
    - Emergency evacuation services for nobles
    - Strategic military assets (troop deployment, supply lines)
- They are rarely warriors; their training is logistical, not tactical
- Combat applications are considered crude and beneath their station
- Most space mages are heavily protected and never see battle

#### 4.2 Standard Space Mage Toolkit (Others)

- **Gates:** Fixed teleportation stations between major hubs; require teams to maintain
- **Teleportation Circles:** Circle-based short or medium-range teleports; requires preparation
- **Gate Artifacts:** Items with embedded formations for limited range point-to-point travel
- **Storage Spaces:** Small pocket dimensions for carrying objects
- Most void mages focus on **stability and safety**, not creativity or efficiency
- Training emphasizes precision and risk-avoidance over innovation

#### 4.3 John's Void Kit

Derived from Casil's library and his own experimentation:

1. **Micro-Gate**
    - Seen as a warm-up exercise by elite space mages; teaching tool for beginners
    - Creates two linked portals (typically hand-sized or smaller)
    - Short range (10-100 meters depending on circle)
    - Low mana cost; can maintain multiple simultaneously at higher circles
    - John weaponizes it tactically:
        - Gate as remote barrel for projectiles
        - Redirects incoming attacks
        - Creates impossible angles of attack
        - Spying and surveillance
        - Theft and sabotage
        - Unraveling enemy spell formations
2. **Basic Teleport**
    - Short-range point-to-point displacement (self only initially)
    - Range increases with circle advancement
    - High mana cost and mental strain
    - Risk of disorientation or spatial overlap (lethal if miscalculated)
    - Used rarely at first; later becomes integrated into close combat
    - By 5th circle, can teleport with reasonable safety within 50-100 meters
3. **Imperfect Internal Pocket Dimension**
    - Library contains only **theoretical** work; considered "impractical dead-end research"
    - Standard pocket dimensions are external (storage rings, etc.) and small
    - John attempts to cast it **inside** his mana pool:
        - Fails to create "infinite internal space" (the theory's goal)
        - Succeeds in forming a **1:1 pocket dimension** that mirrors the size of his mana pool
        - Acts as **extra walls / reinforcement** around his mana
        - Stabilizes shell and delays catastrophic overload
        - Allows him to store far more mana than his body should hold
    - This is completely unprecedented; even Casil doesn't fully understand what John did
    - Becomes critical for crossing the 0.9 → 1.0 evolution threshold
    - Turns him into a living **void battery** with absurd capacity

#### 4.4 Tactical Paradigm – "Human War-Brain + Void"

John treats void magic as a **weapons platform**, not a transport service.

**Core Principle:** Modern infantry tactics + impossible geometry

Examples:

- **Gate-Barrel Sniping**
    - One gate near his hand or weapon, the other near a target's head/joint
    - Fires bullets, throws pebbles, or shoots crossbow bolts through combined gates
    - Impossible trajectories; defenders can't use cover effectively
    - Can shoot around corners, through walls, from behind total concealment
- **Spell Redirection**
    - Opens micro-gates near hostile spells:
        - Reroutes them back to senders (spell reflection)
        - Dumps them away from the tower or into dead space
        - Disrupts spell formations by opening gates inside them (collapses the structure)
    - Makes him extremely difficult to hit with magic
- **Insertion & Theft**
    - Slips small gates into armor cracks, pockets, or behind wards:
        - Teleports bombs, acid, or poison inside defenses
        - Steals keys, artifacts, or documents silently
        - Sabotages equipment (cut a strap, remove a pin)
    - Bypasses most defenses since they're designed against direct assault
- **Field Control**
    - Controls lines of fire without exposing his body
    - Fights entire skirmishes from behind walls or deep within the tower
    - Uses gates to extend his sensory awareness (look through gates like cameras)
    - Creates kill-zones where every angle is covered by gate-redirected projectiles
- **Unraveling**
    - Advanced technique (4th circle+)
    - Opens micro-gates **inside** enemy spell structures
    - The spatial distortion causes mana patterns to collapse
    - Can dismantle formations, wards, and sustained spells
    - Extremely mana-efficient compared to brute-force counter-spelling

**Structure**

- Central institution of magical research and control in the Magic Galactic Empire
- Hierarchy:
    - Grand Archmages (9th circle, extremely rare)
    - High Archmages (8th circle, inner council)
    - Archmages (7th circle, research heads and administrators)
    - Masters (6th circle, senior researchers)
    - Various lower ranks

**Functions**

- Guards high-circle knowledge (6th-9th circle theory and techniques)
- Conducts magical research (weapons, formations, dimensional studies)
- Trains elite mages (selective admission, political connections required)
- Manages strategic assets (void mages, ancient artifacts, ley-line networks)
- Advises Empire on magical matters (military, infrastructure, policy)

**Resources**

- Holds practical knowledge and resources for **6th-9th circle** progression
- Controls access to rare materials, artifacts, and locations
- Maintains gate network across Empire
- Has authority to requisition assets from lower mages

**Interest in John**

- Sees him as a **Living Strategic Gate**:
    - Mobile, thinking space-transport infrastructure
    - Can be deployed anywhere
    - Self-maintaining (feeds on ambient mana)
    - Potentially controllable through contracts or compulsions
- Plans to use him for military logistics or VIP transport
- Completely underestimates his tactical creativity and independence
- Deal with Casil: John for resources and 8th circle support

### 2. The Human Alliance

**Structure**

- Multi-system government spanning dozens of Death Worlds
- Military-focused culture (hostile environments breed toughness)

**Status**

## VI. SPECIAL TECH & ARTIFACTS

### 1. The Home Artifact (Tower Jump Device)

**Visual Design**

- Core: ~1x1 ft transparent crystal cube containing levitating inner sphere
- Aesthetic: Beautiful Elven runic work (flowing, organic patterns)
- Modification: **Ugly iron chains and welded restraints** – crude human engineering bolted onto pristine artifact
- Contrast: Elegance ruined by functionality (perfect metaphor for John's approach)

**Function**

- **Blind Long-Range Teleportation**:
    - Connects to tower's ley-lines and defensive formations
    - Draws enormous mana from tower, surroundings, and ley-lines
    - Rips tower out of current spatial position
    - Deposits it in random location within broad constraints
- **Calibration:**
    - John tunes it to maximize chance of: mana-rich region, outside Empire control, survivable environment
    - Cannot guarantee specific destination (too much distance, too little precision)
    - Risk: Could land in deadly location (star, gas giant, void)
- **Mana Cost:**
    - Requires more mana than any individual could provide
    - Drains ambient mana for miles around activation site

## XII. WRITING GUIDELINES

### **Prose Style:**

- **Sentence Length:** Vary, but favor short and direct for action
- **Paragraph Length:** Short for action, longer for introspection
- **Technical Precision:** Clear explanations of magic/tactics without info-dumping
- **Show vs Tell:** Show in critical moments, tell for time-skips or transitions

### **Action Scenes:**

- **Clarity:** Reader always knows where everyone is and what's happening
- **Tactics:** Show John's thought process, why he makes each move
- **Consequences:** Injuries matter, mana depletion matters, mistakes cost
- **Pacing:** Fast but not rushed, detailed but not bogged down

### **Character Voice:**

- **John:** Terse, efficient, occasional dry humor, military precision
- **Casil:** Formal, arrogant when secure, desperate when threatened
- **Internal Monologue:** John's thoughts should feel tactical, calculating
- **Dialogue:** Minimal, impactful, no wasted words

### **Magic Descriptions:**

- **Visual:** What does it look like? (Colors, shapes, distortions)
- **Sensory:** What does it feel like? (Pressure, temperature, texture)
- **Mechanical:** How does it work? (Mana flow, patterns, structures)
- **Avoid:** Physics jargon, over-explanation, abstract hand-waving
